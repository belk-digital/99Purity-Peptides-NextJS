## What it is

Research-grade peptide reagents for laboratory and analytical use, positioned for an aesthetics-conscious, female-skewing audience interested in longevity, beauty, and self-optimization research.

Compliance positioning: **Research Use Only (RUO).** All product copy maintains this designation. See `/docs/LEGAL-USA.md` for required disclaimers.

## Brand vision

> **"This is backed by science"** AND **"This makes me feel expensive."**

The tension between those two statements *is* the brand. Most peptide vendors lean heavily into one and abandon the other:
- The masculine research-lab vendors (99purity, Limitless Life) lean into clinical credibility but feel cold and forum-coded
- The wellness-influencer vendors lean into lifestyle but lose scientific authority

The Looksmaxxing Lab sits in the gap: as rigorous as Dr. Sturm, as desirable as Rhode, as cinematic as Timeline.

## Voice & tone

### Voice traits

| Trait | What it means | What it isn't |
|---|---|---|
| **Considered** | Every word chosen, never filler | Wordy, padded |
| **Scientific** | Precise terminology used correctly | Jargon-heavy or showing off |
| **Quiet confidence** | States facts; does not sell | Hyperbolic, "amazing!", exclamation points |
| **Editorial** | Reads like a magazine column | Like a product description |
| **Warm** | First-person plural ("we"), human | Robotic, corporate |
| **Feminine without explanation** | Comfortable in its audience | Pandering, "for her", pink |

### Voice examples

**On a product card:**
- ✅ "A stabilized GHRH analog studied for its effects on visceral adipose tissue."
- ❌ "🔥 BURN FAT FAST with our amazing peptide!"
- ❌ "Buy now for a slimmer you!"

**On the homepage hero:**
- ✅ "Research-grade peptides. Documented purity."
- ✅ "Where biotechnology meets ritual."
- ❌ "The #1 trusted peptide store!"

**In an email:**
- ✅ "Your order has shipped. Expect delivery in 2–3 business days."
- ❌ "Yay! Your goodies are on the way 💕"

**On an empty cart:**
- ✅ "Your cart is empty. Begin with our most-studied compounds."
- ❌ "Oops! Nothing here yet 😢 Add something!"

### Words to use

- "Compound," "reagent," "analyte" — not "supplement," "vitamin"
- "Documented," "verified," "characterized" — not "proven," "guaranteed"
- "Studied," "investigated," "researched" — not "shown to," "known to" (avoid efficacy claims)
- "Ritual," "considered," "intentional" — emotional/aesthetic register
- "Clinic," "laboratory" — never "store," "shop" in editorial copy (acceptable in nav: "Shop")

### Words to avoid

- "Buy," "purchase" → use "order"
- "Amazing," "incredible," "best" — superlatives without substance
- "Healing," "treats," "cures" — FDA-claim territory, illegal for RUO products
- "Bro," "stack," "cycle" — masculine peptide-forum vocabulary
- "Hack," "biohack" — overused, juvenile
- "Game-changer," "revolutionary"
- Any emoji in body copy. Ever.

## Brand mark / logo

### Primary identity elements (visible on every product)

1. **Wordmark:** "THE LOOKSMAXXING LAB" — sans-serif, all caps, generous letter-tracking
2. **Face profile:** A single-line champagne-gold drawing of a woman's profile (right-facing). This is the brand's distinctive mark. Use it consistently — never recolor, never crop, never reverse.
3. **Tagline strip:** "BIOTECHNOLOGY | PRECISION | PURITY" — small caps sans-serif, separated by thin vertical bars

### Logo lockup variations

| Variant | Use case |
|---|---|
| **Wordmark only** | Header navigation, email, favicon |
| **Wordmark + face profile (stacked)** | Footer, packaging, business cards |
| **Face profile only** | Favicon, social avatars, watermarks |
| **Tagline strip** | Footer, packaging, occasionally below hero |

### Clear space & minimum size

- Clear space around wordmark: at least the height of one capital letter on all sides
- Minimum legible size for wordmark: **24px** (web), **18mm** (print)
- Minimum size for face profile: **32px**

### What never to do with the logo

- Never put it on a busy photographic background without a tinted overlay
- Never use it in a color other than near-black on light, or cream on dark
- Never animate the face profile (it's a static mark, not a logo intro)
- Never combine with other marks or decorative elements
- Never set it in any typeface other than the official wordmark face

## Positioning & target audience

### Audience description

**Primary:** Women, 28–45, household income $100k+, US-based, interested in:
- Skincare beyond drugstore brands (familiar with Dr. Sturm, Rhode, Augustinus Bader)
- Wellness/longevity (familiar with Dr. Peter Attia, Andrew Huberman, but more aesthetic-leaning)
- Self-care as a considered ritual (Aesop, Le Labo, Maison Margiela energy)
- Doing their own research before purchasing

**Secondary:** Aesthetics-focused men in the same income bracket. Don't redesign for them — they're picking up a product designed for the primary audience.

### Why customers choose us over competitors

1. **Documented purity:** ≥99% HPLC-verified, COA on every order
2. **Considered design:** They feel comfortable having this on their bathroom shelf (most peptide bottles look like meth lab supplies)
3. **Editorial tone:** Information presented with respect for the reader's intelligence
4. **Aesthetic credibility:** Looks at home alongside their other premium beauty/wellness purchases

### What we never compete on

- Lowest price (we will lose, and it positions us wrong)
- Marketing-claim aggressiveness (legally risky and brand-incongruent)
- Influencer hype cycles (we don't chase TikTok trends)

## Photography & imagery direction

### Product photography

- **Surface:** Warm marble, brushed metal, ribbed/fluted glass props, never pure white seamless
- **Lighting:** Soft, directional, with visible reflections on the vial. High-key but not flat.
- **Composition:** Centered or rule-of-thirds, never tilted, never floating-on-white
- **Color cast:** Warm — slight champagne/cream tone throughout
- **Sample reference:** Existing TB-500 and NAD+ shots (see client uploads) — match this exact aesthetic

### Lifestyle imagery

- **Subjects:** Hands, hair, water, glass, fabric — texture-focused. Faces optional and only partial (the side of a face, a hand to a temple). Never a full smiling face — that's beauty-ad cliché.
- **Subjects' bodies:** Diverse, real-looking, not airbrushed. Skin texture visible.
- **Color palette:** Cream, sand, bone, champagne, occasional soft sage or warm blush. Never saturated colors.
- **Settings:** Minimalist interiors, marble surfaces, soft daylight, never sun-flared. Bathrooms, vanities, studios.
- **Avoid:** Stock photography looks. Group shots. Anyone holding a vial like a phone selfie.

### Scientific imagery

- **Molecular structures:** Rendered cleanly, art-directed, in muted gold or near-black against cream. Never neon, never glowing.
- **Lab equipment:** Real glassware, pipettes, HPLC printouts — beautiful but real. No CGI lab fantasy.
- **Reference:** Dr. Sturm's molecular visuals are the ceiling — aim for that quality.

## Brand storytelling pillars

When writing About copy, blog headers, or hero taglines, return to one of these three pillars:

### 1. Documented purity
Every batch HPLC-verified. COAs published. No mystery dosing.

### 2. Considered ritual
Peptide research as part of an intentional, aesthetic-focused lifestyle. Beauty as biology.

### 3. Quiet science
Not loud, not hype-driven. We let the data speak.
